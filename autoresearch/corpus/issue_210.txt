P3: Wire volume into PairsEngine — RVOL filter + ADV gate

## Parent
Tracked in #206 (P3)

## Problem

Volume is fetched from Alpaca, parsed into `StreamBar`, then **dropped** at the `on_bar()` signature. PairsEngine has zero volume awareness. Meanwhile, S&P 500 has 100× liquidity variation between most and least liquid constituents.

SingleEngine already has full RVOL architecture — PairsEngine has none.

## Fix (3 parts)

### Part 1: Wire volume through PairsEngine
- `on_bar(symbol, ts, close, volume)` → propagate to `on_price()`
- Track 20-bar rolling mean volume per leg

### Part 2: Per-leg RVOL entry filter
- Compute RVOL = current_volume / rolling_mean_volume
- Block entry if either leg has RVOL < 0.5 (illiquid conditions)
- Both researchers agree: volume is a FILTER, not a z-score component

### Part 3: ADV gate at pair-picker
- Add minimum ADV ($20M daily dollar volume) to `validate_pair()` in pair-picker
- Reject pairs where either leg is too illiquid to trade at our notional

## Evidence

- **Avellaneda & Lee (2010)**: volume-time OU model achieved Sharpe 1.51 vs ~0.9 without — +0.6 Sharpe from volume awareness
- **Gatev et al. (2006)**: restricted to liquid stocks, noted "nontrivial portion of profits may be due to bid-ask bounce" for illiquid names
- **Almgren & Chriss (2000)**: market impact scales as √(participation rate)
