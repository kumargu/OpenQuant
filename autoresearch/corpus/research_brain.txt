# OpenQuant Research Brain

Distilled knowledge from V1 experiments and prior research. Read this before every experiment.

## V1 Best Config (avg_bps=+13.4, 186 trades, 54.3% WR)
- entry_z=2.0, exit_z=0.5, stop_z=6.0, min_hold=1
- lookback floor=20 bars, ADF: calm=0.10, volatile=0.05

## What We Proved Works
- **Higher entry threshold**: 2.0 sigma, not 1.0. Below 2.0, extra trades are losers.
- **Wider stops**: 6.0 sigma stop gives spreads room to overshoot before reverting.
- **Earlier exits**: 0.5 sigma exit captures bulk of move without risking reversal.
- **Stable z-scores**: Floor of 20 bars for lookback. Below 20, fast-pair z-scores are noise.
- **Looser screening**: ADF p<0.10 in calm, p<0.05 in volatile. The 0.05/0.01 thresholds over-filtered valid pairs.
- **Structural break gate**: Keep it. Pairs with breaks ARE genuinely broken.

## What We Proved Doesn't Work
- entry_z=1.5: dead zone. Trades at 1.5-2.0 sigma are mostly losers.
- entry_z=2.5: too few trades (100 vs 186). Marginal quality gain doesn't offset lost volume.
- max_hold_bars config: dead knob. Per-pair override always wins.
- lookback floor=15: too low, fast-pair noise returns.
- Removing structural break gate: admits genuinely broken pairs.
- HL-heavy scoring (40% weight): fast HL ≠ profitable.
- min_hold=3: forces some day-1 winners to become losers.

## Regime Pattern (Critical)
Strategy alternates good/bad months based on intra-sector dispersion:
- **GOOD** (high sector correlation): May, Jul, Aug, Oct, Jan, Feb, Mar
- **BAD** (high sector dispersion): Jun, Sep, Nov
- Bad months have MORE trades (over-trading into bad regime)
- No dispersion gate exists — biggest open opportunity

## Pair Utilization Problem
- 13,573 candidates → 645 loaded → 78 traded (12% utilization)
- 567 selected pairs NEVER trade. 88% waste.
- GOOG/GOOGL (same company) traded 21 times — should be excluded
- Top winners are financials (AXP/BAC, COF/JPM, GS/JPM)
- MCO/NTRS lost -733 bps over 4 trades — per-pair learning needed

## Open Research Questions (V2 Targets)
1. Intra-sector dispersion gate (suppress entries when sector is dispersed)
2. Per-pair learning (stop trading a pair after 2-3 consecutive losses)
3. Same-company exclusion (GOOG/GOOGL, dual-class shares)
4. Volume/liquidity gating
5. Rolling hedge ratio (Kalman filter)
6. Better pair utilization (why select pairs that never trade?)
7. Sector-aware position sizing (lean into financials which work best)
